# Rationale — Cycle 007 — strawman

Created: 2026-05-25T01:01:03.784888+00:00

## Summary

The cycle 006 adversarial pass identified nine structural failure modes and several dead ends, but left multiple underdeveloped areas that the proposal gestures at without fully exploring: the alternative framings section lists five but misses two important structural variants; graduated delegation is a single paragraph that doesn't develop its architectural implications; the governance model addresses standards bodies but not platform ownership; and the reversibility dead end for criterion 3 was flagged without a proposed resolution. This pass adds those missing branches, names two new governance-level concepts (exit ecosystem, user cooperative), proposes legible-defaults as a reframing of the criterion 3 dead end, and introduces regulatory alignment as an underexplored bootstrap strategy for freedom assessment.

## Changes Made

- **Alternative Framings**: Updated count from five to seven; added exit-option framing (freedom as credible exit availability rather than active exercise) and active-governance framing (freedom as collective shaping power rather than individual rights)
- **Graduated delegation expansion**: Extended the single [speculative] paragraph into a composable sovereignty section with a decomposition matrix mapping the five GNU freedoms to architectural delegation decisions; identified unbundling as a positive architectural obligation
- **Exit ecosystem (Right to Fork)**: Added a named [speculative] section identifying that individual exit rights require a viable ecosystem of alternatives — availability, viability, and discoverability — as a necessary condition for the exit option to remain credible
- **User cooperative governance**: Added [speculative] section in Governance Model presenting platform cooperative model as ownership-based alternative to multi-stakeholder standards bodies, with federated cooperative extension
- **Freedom budget (Architectural Implications)**: Added [speculative] preferred characteristic treating architectural freedom-cost as a running account rather than a point-in-time property
- **Legible defaults (Reversibility)**: Added [speculative] alternative framing after criterion 3 dead end; proposes separating the disclosure obligation (must communicate reversibility status) from the reversibility obligation (must be reversible), allowing security-by-design irreversible defaults while preserving user awareness
- **Regulatory alignment (Freedom Decay)**: Added new [speculative] subsection identifying convergent regulatory requirements (GDPR Article 20, SBOM mandates, right-to-repair, DMA interoperability) as incidental freedom-preservation conditions; frames this as a bootstrap strategy for reducing marginal cost of freedom assessment
- **Decision Record**: Added cycle 007 entry

## Why These Changes Matter

The existing document has developed the failure modes and constraints of freedom-preservation thoroughly through three adversarial passes, but the solution space remains narrow. The core principles are well-defended but brittle — each faces at least one structural dead end. Strawman generativity serves two functions here: (1) introducing genuinely different framings that might survive the adversarial critiques applied to existing framings (exit-option framing sidesteps the individual-capacity problem; user-cooperative sidesteps governance-capture via ownership); (2) providing partial resolution paths for existing dead ends rather than merely leaving them flagged (legible-defaults is a defensible weakening of criterion 3; regulatory alignment provides a funded mechanism for assessment without requiring dedicated governance). The composable sovereignty matrix also makes the graduated delegation concept concrete enough for the next steelman pass to either promote it or find a specific structural objection.
