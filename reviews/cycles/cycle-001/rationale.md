## Summary

The seed document presents a coherent but monolithic case for freedom-preserving systems that needs more surface area before it can be steelmanned or challenged effectively. This pass adds alternative framings of the core premise, splits two overloaded concepts (user sovereignty, modifiability), names several emergent anti-patterns absent from the original, and surfaces six unresolved tensions the document collapses or ignores. The goal is to expand the solution space before it contracts around the GNU-derived framing.

## Changes Made

- Added "Alternative Framings of the Premise" section after the premise, introducing instrumentalist, commons, capability, and anti-domination framings as alternative lenses
- Split "User Sovereignty" into individual vs. collective sovereignty; added the "who is the user" open question for organizational contexts
- Split "Modifiability" into formal freedom vs. practical freedom; named the "complexity moat" anti-pattern; added speculative positive obligations on system builders
- Added "fork fragmentation" tension to the Right to Fork section
- Expanded Anti-Patterns with seven new entries: cloudization, network capture, training data extraction, permissive-to-proprietary pipeline, complexity moat, API capture, composability paradox
- Added "Open Tensions" section with six named tensions (freedom/security, formal/practical freedom, individual/collective sovereignty, sustainability, negative/positive liberty, AI systems)
- Added "Questions for Steelman Pass" to explicitly pass unresolved questions forward

## Why These Changes Matter

The seed document is well-argued but operates almost entirely in the register of negative liberty (freedom *from* restriction) derived from the GNU definition. This creates a blind spot: it doesn't address what makes freedom practically exercisable, who bears positive obligations, how collective and individual freedoms interact, or whether the framework survives contact with AI systems. By naming these gaps explicitly rather than letting them stay implicit, the next pass can choose to reinforce, extend, or challenge the framework on solid ground. The new anti-patterns (especially cloudization, network capture, and training data extraction) reflect freedom-reduction patterns that postdate the GNU formulation and are practically relevant to modern system design.
